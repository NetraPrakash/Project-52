Origami is the art of paper folding from Japan. It comes from the Japanese words 'ori', meaning 'folding', and 'kami', meaning 'paper'.

The origami crane is the most popular example of origami. Traditionally, folding a thousand paper cranes is said to grant you the right to make one special wish.

Origami generally discourages the use of cuts, glue, or markings on the paper. Most people fold the paper with their hands, rather than using other tools.

Origami is now famous all over the world.
